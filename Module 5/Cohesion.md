# Cohesion

Cohesion measures how closely related the responsibilities inside a software unit are, from methods in a class to modules, services, and larger subsystems.

High cohesion means elements work together on one clear purpose and improves maintainability, reusability, testability, and clarity, while low cohesion mixes unrelated responsibilities and makes systems harder to understand and evolve.
