# Architectural Design Principles

Architectural design principles are practical guidelines for building software that is maintainable, scalable, cost-aware, and efficient, with emphasis on clean structure and clear responsibilities across a system.

Key principles include cohesion and coupling, single responsibility, least knowledge (Law of Demeter), and stateful vs stateless design, all of which help teams create consistent architectures that are easier to understand, evolve, and debug.
