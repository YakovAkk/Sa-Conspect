# Technology-Agnostic Architecture

> [!note] Overview
> ==Tech agnosticism== is an approach that allows developers and engineers to work **without strong preferences for specific tools or technologies**. This gives them more freedom to build, connect, and manage applications in ways that best fit business needs.
>
> Technology-agnostic architecture also relates to the ==Internet of Things (IoT)==. It supports connecting all parts of an IoT system, including device management, communication, data analysis, integration, and application support.

## Layer Breakdown

### Application / Business Layer

At the **top** of the architecture, the IoT platform layer connects with the applications and business layer. This layer contains applications that offer end-users a productive and useful view of the IoT platform's functionalities.

---

### Cyber Scope

> [!info] Cyber Scope
> The ==cyber scope== spans **two environments**: **Cloud** and **Client**.
>
> The ==IoT platform== is the most beneficial part of the architecture. It is the key to supporting essential features:
> - Device management
> - Interoperation
> - Security
> - Reusability
> - Control of enormous volumes of data

#### Data Layer

The **data layer** of the IoT platform stores all data generated by the system, divided into two categories:

| Data Category | Contents |
|---|---|
| Data for **microservices** | Observations and alert data |
| Data for **serverless functions** | Ancillary data required to perform serverless functions |

#### Services Layer

The **services layer** is an IoT platform component composed of **six sub-layers**:

> [!info] Six Sub-Layers of the Services Layer
> 1. Serverless Functions
> 2. APIs
> 3. Microservices
> 4. Persistence
> 5. Analytics
> 6. Broker

##### Serverless Functions

Defines two capabilities:
- **User management** — registration, authentication, discovery, and removal of system users.
- **Device management** — the same functionalities applied to IoT devices.

##### Persistence Layer

The **persistence layer** is used by Ingestion or Query microservices to store **permanent data** from the perception layer and other generated data for further analysis or recovery.

##### Microservices

Four core microservices in the platform:

| Microservice | Role |
|---|---|
| **Ingestion** | Collects data originating from IoT devices |
| **Query** | Retrieves historical data from IoT devices |
| **Alert** | Collects and manages alerts |
| **Devices** | Connects to IoT devices to ensure network integrity |

> [!tip] Scalable by Design
> The number of microservices can increase depending on the solution's needs.

##### Analytics

The **analytics module** analyzes data, launches specific events/alerts, or detects particular behavior patterns within the IoT data stream.

##### Broker

> [!info] Broker
> The ==broker== is the **entry point** to the IoT platform. It:
> - Connects with various IoT devices using **multiple protocols**.
> - Acts as a link between the IoT platform logic and IoT devices.
> - **Receives** data from edge devices and **sends** data back to them.
> - Offers a **message queuing system**, providing delivery order, redundancy, and interface decoupling.

---

### Physical Scope

> [!info] Physical Scope
> The ==physical scope== represents IoT devices from a **hardware perspective** and includes two layers:
> - **Network layer** — handles device communication and connectivity.
> - **Perception layer** — covers the IoT devices themselves and their capabilities (sensors, actuators, etc.).

## Benefits of Technology Agnosticism

Being technology-agnostic offers key benefits for developers and organizations:

> [!tip] Why Technology Agnosticism Matters
> - **Flexible solutions** — developers can build for customers regardless of the IT systems they already use.
> - **Scalability** — the platform is not locked into one vendor's scaling approach.
> - **Stability** — avoiding tight dependency on a single technology reduces risk of obsolescence.
> - **Reusability** — components and patterns can be reused across different technology stacks.

Technology-agnostic IoT platforms play a key role in connecting devices and applications, helping to improve scalability, stability, and reusability of the system.

## Summary

Technology-agnostic architecture provides a flexible foundation for IoT systems by avoiding lock-in to any specific tool or vendor. Its layered structure — from physical devices through the broker and services layer up to the application layer — cleanly separates concerns across hardware, data, business logic, and presentation. This separation, combined with modular microservices and serverless functions, makes the platform adaptable, scalable, and reusable across diverse deployment environments.
