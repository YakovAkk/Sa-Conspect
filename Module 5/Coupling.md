# Coupling

Coupling describes how strongly software parts depend on one another and how changes in one unit can impact others across methods, classes, modules, services, or systems.

Strong coupling creates tight runtime dependencies, while loose coupling improves modularity, scalability, testability, and makes it easier to evolve or replace parts without disrupting the whole system.
