By prioritizing capabilities over processes, businesses can create

Solution requirements define the capabilities and qualities a solution must possess to fulfill
stakeholder needs. They provide detailed features and behaviors expected from the system,
guiding the development team during implementation.

1. Functional Requirements - functions that business product(application) has
   Examples:
   • Display customer last name as a link to account history.
   • Allow sorting by account opening date.
2. Non-Functional Requirements specify quality attributes the system must meet
   Examples:
   • Allow up to 200 concurrent users.
   • Require strong passwords of at least eight characters in length containing a minimum
   of one non-alphabet character.

Also requirements have clasification

1. Business requirements - the requirements are often related to customers.
   These objectives focus on what the business needs at a high level
   Example:
   • Reduce incorrectly processed orders by 50% by the end of next quarter.
   • Increase repeat orders from customers by 10% within six months after deployment.
2. Stakeholder requirements articulate the needs of stakeholders that must align with business objectives.
   Examples:
   • Add new customer account.
   • Create new order/Check order status.
3. Transition requirements define the capabilities needed to move, deploy, or adapt a
   solution to a new platform or environment. When implementing a solution,
   these requirements ensure that the transition can be completed successfully.
   Examples:
   • The solution must run on all Java platforms, including 64-bit versions.
   • Users must complete an online certification before they are allowed to use the system.

RAID(risks, assumptions, issues, and dependencies) are other crucial aspects to
manage in projects in addition to requirements.

1. Risk - future problems which may occure if a certan solution is done
2. Assumptions are agreed-upon facts that may influence the initiative.
3. Issues encompass anything that should be taken into account.
4. Dependencies refer to connections between different parts of the solution.
