# Presales Process: Stages from Qualification to Retrospective

> [!note] Overview
> Solution architects are usually **not involved at the very beginning** of a project (initial contact, lead generation) — that work belongs to the ==presales team and top management==. Architects join as the process advances and technical depth becomes needed. The presales process itself is a structured journey of six stages, each critical for winning the deal and aligning with client expectations.

## 1. Qualification & Kick-off

The initiation of the presales journey. The team sets up the engagement structurally before any technical work begins.

> [!info] Key Activities
> - **Opportunity Registration**
> - **Kick-off meetings**
> - Assigning team **roles and responsibilities**
> - Studying the ==RFP package== to understand client requirements thoroughly

This stage answers: *who is doing what, and what is the client really asking for?*

## 2. Initial Estimations and Meeting with Customer

After qualification, the team produces a first-pass technical and effort view of the work.

> [!info] Key Activities
> - Conduct **Initial Estimations**
> - Formulate **questions for the customer**
> - Seek **clarifications** on ambiguous points
> - Schedule a meeting with the customer
> - Optionally **demonstrate** proposed solutions

> [!tip] Why Early Meetings Matter
> Direct contact uncovers unstated requirements and confirms assumptions before deep proposal work begins.

## 3. Proposal Preparation

The most labor-intensive stage. The team builds the full proposal package.

> [!info] Key Activities
> - Gather **Generic Information**
> - Document **Assumptions**
> - Outline **Solution / Approach** strategies
> - **Refine Estimations**
> - Define **Team / Timeline** structures
> - Prepare **Case Studies and References**
> - **Integrate** all proposal components
> - Review and update the proposal document

This is where content development, estimations, and architectural input come together into a single artifact.

## 4. Pre-final Review

Before submission, the proposal undergoes ==thorough scrutiny==.

> [!info] Key Activities
> - **Integrate** various components into a cohesive whole
> - **Validate** estimations and proposals for **consistency**
> - **Polish** the document for clarity and coherence

> [!warning] Don't Skip This Stage
> Inconsistencies between sections (e.g., timeline vs. estimates) damage credibility. The pre-final review exists specifically to catch them.

## 5. Proposal Submission

The proposal is formally delivered to the client.

> [!example] What This Stage Marks
> - **Culmination** of all preparation work
> - **Beginning** of the client's evaluation process
> - The vendor's pitch is now in the client's hands

## 6. Retrospective

After submission, the team reflects on the process — regardless of outcome.

> [!info] Key Activities
> - Gather and analyze **Customer Feedback**
> - Identify **Lessons Learned**
> - Strategize for **future presales endeavors**

Retrospective is the engine that improves presales capability over time.

## Architect's Entry Point

> [!tip] When Architects Join
> Solution architects typically enter the process **after qualification** — once the RFP is understood and other team members have done initial setup. By that point, technical depth (estimations, solution design) is the next bottleneck, and the architect is brought in to unblock it.

## Stages at a Glance

| Stage | Primary Focus | Key Output |
|---|---|---|
| Qualification & Kick-off | Setup & RFP study | Roles assigned, RFP understood |
| Initial Estimations & Customer Meeting | First sizing & clarifications | Early estimate, open questions resolved |
| Proposal Preparation | Building the proposal | Full proposal draft |
| Pre-final Review | Consistency & polish | Reviewed, integrated proposal |
| Proposal Submission | Delivery | Submitted proposal |
| Retrospective | Learning | Lessons learned, feedback analysis |

## When Each Stage Adds the Most Value

> [!example] Decision Hints
> - **Skipping qualification** → wasted effort on misaligned proposals.
> - **Skipping early customer meetings** → assumptions get baked into estimates and become hard to remove later.
> - **Skipping pre-final review** → inconsistencies reach the client and damage credibility.
> - **Skipping retrospective** → the next bid repeats the same mistakes.

## Summary

The presales process is a **six-stage structured framework**: Qualification & Kick-off → Initial Estimations & Customer Meeting → Proposal Preparation → Pre-final Review → Proposal Submission → Retrospective. Solution architects typically join after qualification, contributing to estimations, solution design, and reviews. Following each step carefully — and refining strategies through retrospectives — is what allows organizations to improve their presales capabilities and **win more projects while meeting client needs**.
