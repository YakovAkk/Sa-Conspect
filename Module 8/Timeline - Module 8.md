# Timeline: Project Timeline Creation in Pre-Sales

> [!note] Overview
> Creating a project timeline is an important step in pre-sales project management. It helps teams understand **how long the project will take**, assign resources properly, and see how tasks are connected. A well-built timeline improves **planning, teamwork, and communication** — increasing the chances of project success.

## 1. Identify Task Dependencies

Once all project tasks are identified, establish **dependencies** among them.

> [!info] Dependency Categories
> - **Waiting on** — dependent on completion of other tasks
> - **Blocking** — preventing the start of other tasks
> - **Linked to** — related but not strictly dependent

Identifying these dependencies ensures an **accurate timeline**.

## 2. Identify Resources

Recognize the **best available resources** for solution development.

> [!info] Resource Identification
> - Assign resources to the team
> - **Monitor workload** throughout the project
> - Use an **up-to-date resource calendar** for smooth adjustments

## 3. Identify Important Milestones

Milestones mark the **completion of one phase** and the **beginning of the next**.

> [!info] Milestone Properties
> - Achieved by completing task sequences and deliverables
> - Serve as **crucial markers** in the timeline
> - Guide project progress
> - Useful for client communication and contract gates

## 4. Create, Review, and Optimize Timeline

Build the timeline, then refine it.

> [!info] Steps in This Phase
> - **Sequence tasks** to reflect allocated time
> - **Add milestones** to signify key project phases
> - **Review** for alignment with resource allocation
> - Consider **time, quality, and cost** constraints
> - Stakeholders must balance **deadlines, quality, and resource optimization**

## Timeline Creation at a Glance

| Step | Key Question Answered |
|---|---|
| Identify Task Dependencies | What blocks what? |
| Identify Resources | Who does each task? |
| Identify Milestones | What are the key checkpoints? |
| Create, Review, Optimize | Does the whole plan hold together? |

## 5. What the Finished Timeline Shows

> [!example] Typical Visual Output
> - Workstreams (parallel tracks of work)
> - Sub-phases within each workstream
> - **Task dependencies** (arrows or links)
> - **Milestones** as anchor points

## Why Timelines Matter

> [!tip] Strategic Reasons
> - **Resource efficiency** — visibility prevents over-allocation.
> - **Risk reduction** — dependencies surface earlier.
> - **Stakeholder alignment** — everyone sees the same plan.
> - **Negotiation tool** — explicit milestones help during contract discussions.

## Common Pitfalls

> [!warning] What Breaks Timelines
> - **Missing dependencies** — invisible blockers ruin the schedule.
> - **Optimistic durations** — underestimating tasks cascades.
> - **No buffer** — first slip cascades into the rest.
> - **Stale resource calendar** — leads to double-booking.

## Summary

Building a pre-sales project timeline follows four steps: **identify task dependencies**, **identify resources**, **identify milestones**, and **create, review, and optimize** the timeline. The result is a visual plan showing workstreams, sub-phases, and dependencies that improves planning, teamwork, and communication — meaningfully raising the chance of project success.
