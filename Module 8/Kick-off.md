# Kick-off: Initial Phase of the RFP Process

> [!note] Overview
> The ==kick-off phase== is the initial step in the RFP process. It sets the base for **good teamwork and cooperation with stakeholders**. The team organizes resources, defines roles and tasks, opens communication lines, and prepares for a smooth project process.

## 1. Compose Team

Assemble a **diverse and skilled** project team with relevant expertise and experience.

> [!info] Team Composition Goals
> - Cover the technical and business breadth of the RFP
> - Clarify **team roles**, **responsibilities**, and **project duration expectations**
> - Match seniority and skills to the work ahead

## 2. Process Visibility

Make the work observable for everyone involved.

> [!info] Three Visibility Practices
> - **Create Schedule** — outline key milestones, deadlines, and deliverables.
> - **Schedule Status Calls** — regular meetings to review progress, address challenges, and stay aligned.
> - **Align Responsibilities (RACI)** — RACI matrix clarifying who is **R**esponsible, **A**ccountable, **C**onsulted, and **I**nformed.

## 3. Share Materials

Use collaborative platforms to manage RFP artifacts.

> [!info] Common Platforms
> - **SharePoint**
> - **Google Drive**
> - Similar collaborative tools
>
> Ensure all team members can access documents, templates, and resources.

## 4. Start Working on Questions and Design

Begin substantive work in parallel with setup.

> [!info] Early Substantive Work
> - Brainstorm questions and insights about RFP requirements
> - Draft initial designs, proposals, or outlines
> - Encourage open communication and collaboration
> - Foster creativity and innovation around RFP challenges

## Kick-off Actions at a Glance

| Action | Goal | Key Artifact |
|---|---|---|
| Compose Team | Right people, right skills | Team roster + roles |
| Process Visibility | Track and align | Schedule, RACI, recurring calls |
| Share Materials | Easy access | SharePoint / Drive workspace |
| Questions & Design | Start the real work | Question backlog, draft designs |

## Why Kick-off Matters

> [!tip] Strategic Reasons
> - **Avoid mistakes** later by clarifying ownership upfront.
> - **Smooth workflow** — communication channels are open from day one.
> - **Faster ramp-up** — materials and roles ready when work begins.
> - **Stakeholder confidence** — visible structure inspires trust.

## Summary

The kick-off phase organizes the RFP effort before any deep work begins. Four core actions — **compose the team, establish process visibility (schedule, status calls, RACI), share materials on collaborative platforms, and start working on questions and design** — set clear roles, tasks, and communication paths. Skipping or rushing kick-off almost always shows up later as missed deadlines or miscommunication.
