# Overall Timeline: Project Lifecycle from Architect's Perspective

> [!note] Overview
> The outsourced software development process spans multiple stages with many stakeholders. From the ==Solution Architect's== perspective, involvement begins as soon as a technical review or design is required — typically in the ==presales phase==, marked by "RF" requests (RFI, RFP, RFQ, RFT). The lifecycle moves through five major stages: **Presales → Contract & Engagement → Discovery → Construction → Transition**, each with distinct architect responsibilities.

## 1. Presales

The project starts with one of the "Request For" (RF) artifacts. These help the vendor understand the client's needs and shape the proposal.

### RF Types
> [!info] Common RF Artifacts
> - **RFI** — Request for Information (capabilities, team size, tech stack)
> - **RFP** — Request for Proposal (goals, architecture, estimation, summary)
> - **RFQ** — Request for Quotation (pricing focus)
> - **RFT** — Request for Tenders (formal competitive bidding)

### Architect Responsibilities
- During ==RFI==: provide information about company capabilities (e.g., number of available .NET developers).
- During ==RFP==: produce **architectural solutions, estimations, and proposal summaries** aligned with the client's goals and expectations.

> [!tip] Why It Matters
> Presales outcomes directly affect how well teams collaborate later. The early discussions shape every downstream phase.

## 2. Contract & Engagement

Once the client commits, the parties formalize the relationship through legal instruments.

### Key Contracts
> [!info] Typical Documents
> - **NDA** — Non-Disclosure Agreement (protects confidential information)
> - **MSA** — Master Service Agreement (overarching terms)
> - **SOW** — Statement of Work (specific tasks and deliverables)

The ==SOW== is especially important: it defines exactly what will be built and delivered.

## 3. Discovery

With contracts in place, deep technical and business work begins.

### Activities
- **Solution Architects** dive deep into project requirements.
- **Business Analysts** refine use cases.
- Close collaboration with the client ensures comprehensive understanding.

### Outputs
> [!example] Deliverables
> - Detailed architectural designs
> - Business use cases
> - Refined scope and assumptions

## 4. Construction

Actual development begins. Use cases identified during discovery are implemented.

### Team Composition
- Developers
- DevOps engineers
- QA personnel
- Architects (oversight, technical guidance, design changes)

This is where the solution moves from documents and diagrams into running software.

## 5. Transition

The project moves to its next lifecycle stage.

### Possible Paths
> [!info] Transition Options
> - **Production rollout** — handover to operations
> - **Next iteration** — continued development
> - **Support / Hypercare** — stabilization period after go-live
> - **Vendor handover** — transferring to a different vendor (due to circumstances or client decisions)

### Critical Activities
- Knowledge transfer sessions
- Quality validation
- Documentation handoff

## Stage Comparison at a Glance

| Stage | Architect's Role | Key Artifacts | Primary Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Presales | Solutions, estimations | RFI/RFP/RFQ/RFT responses | Win the project |
| Contract | Review technical scope | NDA, MSA, SOW | Formalize terms |
| Discovery | Deep design & analysis | Architecture designs, use cases | Define what to build |
| Construction | Oversight & guidance | Working software | Build the solution |
| Transition | KT & quality validation | Handover docs, support plans | Smooth lifecycle move |

## Why the Presales Phase Stands Out

> [!example] Strategic Importance
> - Selling software development services requires **special attention** in presales.
> - Early discussions and the vendor's official proposal **shape the project's success**.
> - Presales outcomes strongly affect later team collaboration.
> - This module dives deeper into the presales phase specifically.

## Summary

The lifecycle of an outsourced software project moves through five distinct stages, each with specific architect involvement. The architect's primary value emerges in ==presales== (shaping the solution and proposal) and ==discovery== (detailed design), with continued oversight throughout construction and transition. Regardless of stage, the goal stays constant: **deliver high-quality results and ensure a smooth transition to the next phase**, providing value and meeting client expectations.
