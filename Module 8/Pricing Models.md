# Pricing Models: Overview of Common Pricing Approaches

> [!note] Overview
> The **pricing model** is inherently tied to the **engagement model**. Staff augmentation typically uses **Time & Materials (T&M)**; consultancy engagements may use either **T&M** or **fixed-price**. Three primary pricing models cover most engagements: **Fixed-Price**, **Outcome-Based**, and **Time and Materials (T&M)**.

## 1. Fixed-Price

The client pays a **predetermined amount** for a **specified scope** of work, regardless of resources used or time spent.

> [!info] Key Traits
> - **Budget predictability** for the client
> - **Resource management** transfers to the service provider
> - Contract outlines scope, **assumptions, responsibilities, and issue resolution**
> - Best for well-defined, stable-scope projects

## 2. Outcome-Based

Payment is tied to **predefined outcomes** or Key Performance Indicators (KPIs).

> [!info] Key Traits
> - Metrics like **team velocity** or **delivered vs. planned user stories** are tracked
> - Failing to meet agreed **SLAs** can lead to **vendor revenue loss**
> - **High risk** for vendor — **high control** for client
> - Aligns incentives between parties

## 3. Time and Materials (T&M)

Payment is based on the **time spent** by the contractor's employees.

> [!info] Key Traits
> - Rates × hours (or days) worked
> - Plus cost of materials
> - **Low risk for service provider**
> - **Change management is crucial** to handle scope variations
> - Some contracts include **budget caps**

## Pricing Models at a Glance

| Model | Risk to Vendor | Client Control | Best When |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fixed-Price | High | Low | Scope is locked |
| Outcome-Based | **Very high** | **Very high** | Outcomes can be measured |
| T&M | Low | Medium | Scope evolves |

## 4. How Pricing Relates to Engagement

> [!example] Common Pairings
> - **Staff Augmentation** → T&M
> - **Offshore Development Center** → T&M
> - **Project-Based Model** → Fixed-Price
> - **Product Development Services** → Outcome-Based
> - **Digital Platform Engineering** → Outcome-Based or hybrid
> - **Consulting** → T&M or Fixed-Price (flexible)

## 5. Selecting the Right Model

> [!tip] Decision Factors
> - **Nature of the engagement** — discovery vs implementation vs maintenance
> - **Scope clarity** — fixed scope favors fixed-price
> - **Risk tolerance** — both sides
> - **Measurability** of outcomes — outcome-based needs clean KPIs
> - **Use case** — specific client requirements

## Common Pitfalls

> [!warning] What Goes Wrong
> - Choosing **fixed-price for evolving scope** — leads to over-runs and quality cuts.
> - Choosing **T&M for highly constrained budgets** — invites unbounded billing.
> - Choosing **outcome-based without measurable KPIs** — invites endless disputes.

## Summary

Three primary pricing models — **Fixed-Price**, **Outcome-Based**, and **Time and Materials** — cover most IT engagements. Each is tightly linked to specific engagement models and project characteristics: **scope clarity, risk tolerance, and measurability of outcomes**. Selecting the right model is a judgment call about which side bears what risk and what behavior the contract should incentivize.
