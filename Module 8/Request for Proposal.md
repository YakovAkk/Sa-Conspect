# Request for Proposal (RFP): Purpose, Timeline and Nine-Step Process

> [!note] Overview
> A ==Request for Proposal (RFP)== is a formal request — often run as a bidding process — from a company or agency seeking to purchase a product, service, or valuable asset. It asks potential suppliers to submit **business proposals**. The RFP is typically sent **early** in the purchasing process, during initial research or at the start of procurement.

## 1. Purpose of an RFP

> [!info] Primary Objective
> Request proposals from potential vendors. After gathering all necessary information, the customer describes requirements (e.g., *"a CRM system for 25,000 users"*), and vendors respond with **complete solutions, delivery plans, and total cost estimates**.

### The Solution Architect's Job
> [!tip] Architect's Core Responsibility
> - Propose a ==complete solution==
> - **Plan its delivery**
> - **Estimate the total cost**

## 2. Typical Duration

> [!info] RFP Timeline at a Glance
> - **Range**: a few weeks to two months
> - **Average**: about **one month**
> - The timeline must be **carefully planned**, with daily tasks organized to finish on time.
> - Each day and action must be planned in detail.

## 3. RFP Timeline Artifacts

Two timeline artifacts are typically prepared and shared:

> [!info] Two Common Timeline Documents
> - **Excel RFP timeline** — covers the full process, shared with stakeholders for alignment.
> - **Solution Architect's timeline** — focuses on architect-specific tasks and deliverables.

> [!tip] Share Early
> Share the timeline with stakeholders so **everyone is aligned and informed**.

## 4. The Nine-Step RFP Process

### 4.1 RFP Issued
The RFP is released to potential vendors.

### 4.2 Intent to Bid / NDA
Before any work or discussions commence, an ==NDA== (Non-Disclosure Agreement) must be signed to **protect both parties' interests**.

### 4.3 Question Submit
Both parties engage in a **bidirectional exchange** of questions and answers to clarify requirements.

### 4.4 Q&A Summary Provided
The company formulates a comprehensive response to the RFP, including:

> [!info] Q&A Summary Contents
> - Executive summaries
> - Solution approaches
> - Architectural diagrams
> - Cost estimates
> - Delivery timelines

### 4.5 RFP Response Submission
The company's response is submitted to the customer.

### 4.6 RFP Evaluation
The customer assesses received proposals and **selects potential vendors** for further discussions.

### 4.7 Discussions, Negotiations, Workshop
Workshops and negotiations address any modifications or clarifications needed.

### 4.8 Final Selection
A final decision is made by the customer, involving multiple departments:

> [!info] Stakeholders in Final Selection
> - **Legal**
> - **Procurement**
> - **Sales**
> - **Delivery**

### 4.9 Contract Signed
If successful, a contract is signed between the parties.

## Nine Steps at a Glance

| # | Step | What Happens |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | RFP Issued | Client releases RFP to vendors |
| 2 | Intent to Bid / NDA | NDA signed before any discussions |
| 3 | Question Submit | Two-way clarifications |
| 4 | Q&A Summary Provided | Vendor builds full response (exec summary, solution, diagrams, costs, timeline) |
| 5 | RFP Response Submission | Vendor delivers proposal |
| 6 | RFP Evaluation | Client shortlists candidates |
| 7 | Discussions, Negotiations, Workshop | Refinement and clarification |
| 8 | Final Selection | Multi-department decision |
| 9 | Contract Signed | Engagement formalized |

## 5. Why Detailed Planning Matters

> [!warning] Tight Deadlines
> RFPs often run on a fixed client deadline. Missing it usually disqualifies the bid. Daily-level planning is not optional — it's the difference between submitting on time and submitting nothing.

> [!tip] Use the Excel File for Coordination
> The Excel timeline should include and explain all **nine steps**. This document becomes the master plan for **all presales activities** related to the RFP.

## 6. RFP vs. RFI vs. RFQ

| Aspect | RFI | RFQ | RFP |
|---|---|---|---|
| Goal | Gather info | Get pricing | Get full proposal |
| Formality | Low | Medium | **Highest** |
| Architect's focus | Technical answers | Resource sizing | **Solution + WBS + cost** |
| Outcome | Vendor shortlist | Selected supplier | Selected partner & contract |
| Duration | Days | Weeks | **Weeks to 2 months** |

## When Architects Engage

> [!example] Architect Involvement Across the Nine Steps
> - **Step 3 (Question Submit)** — formulating clarifying technical questions.
> - **Step 4 (Q&A Summary)** — designing the solution, drawing architectural diagrams, sizing the team.
> - **Step 5 (RFP Response)** — reviewing technical accuracy of the submitted document.
> - **Step 7 (Discussions / Workshops)** — defending and refining the proposed solution.

## Summary

An RFP is the **most formal presales process**. Vendors propose complete solutions, delivery plans, and total cost estimates in response to a client's stated need. The process flows through **nine steps** — from RFP Issued to Contract Signed — and typically takes a few weeks to two months, averaging about one month. Detailed daily planning, shared timeline artifacts (Excel + architect-specific), and active Solution Architect involvement across solution design, estimation, and workshops are what make an RFP response competitive.
