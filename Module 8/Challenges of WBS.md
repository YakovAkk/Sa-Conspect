# Challenges of WBS

> [!note] Overview
> A Work Breakdown Structure gives clear insight into a project's **scope and deliverables**. But building and managing one is not a mechanical exercise — Solution Architects face several recurring challenges, especially around **structure choice**, **validation**, and **keeping the WBS aligned with reality as the project evolves**.

## 1. Deciding on Structure

> [!warning] The Structural Choice
> One of the primary challenges is **how to organize the WBS**. Solution Architects must choose between:
> - **Deliverable-oriented** structure — organized by what gets built (modules, features)
> - **Life cycle / activity-based** structure — organized by what gets done (design, develop, test)

> [!tip] How to Decide
> - Deliverable-oriented suits projects with **clear, modular outputs**.
> - Life-cycle-based suits projects where **process phases dominate**.
> - Many real projects use a **hybrid** — top levels deliverable, lower levels activity.

## 2. Validating with a Bottom-Up Approach

> [!info] Why Bottom-Up Validation
> Validating the **accuracy and completeness** of a top-down WBS is hard. A bottom-up approach addresses this by seeking input from team members to ensure **all necessary tasks and deliverables are included**.

Bottom-up validation typically involves:
- Each specialist listing what they would need to do
- Comparing those lists to the WBS leaves
- Adding missing items or merging duplicates

## 3. Re-evaluating with the Team

> [!warning] The WBS Is Not Frozen
> As the project progresses and new information emerges, the WBS often requires revisions. Collaborating with the project team to **re-evaluate and adjust** the WBS ensures alignment with **evolving project needs and goals**.

> [!tip] When to Re-evaluate
> - After major scope changes
> - After discovery findings
> - When estimates start drifting from reality
> - At major milestones

## Challenges at a Glance

| Challenge | Risk if Ignored | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Deciding on Structure | Confusing or misleading WBS | Pick deliverable vs activity (or hybrid) deliberately |
| Bottom-Up Validation | Missing tasks, undersized estimates | Team-driven completeness check |
| Re-evaluating | WBS drifts from reality | Periodic team review, scheduled revisions |

## 4. Why Addressing These Matters

> [!example] Strategic Reasons
> - **More accurate estimates** — comprehensive WBS prevents systematic under-estimation.
> - **Better timelines** — a current WBS keeps schedules realistic.
> - **Fewer disputes** — explicit scope reduces contract conflicts.
> - **Client confidence** — visible adaptation shows mature delivery practice.

## Summary

The main WBS challenges are **structural choice** (deliverable-oriented vs life-cycle-based), **bottom-up validation** (involving the team to catch missing tasks), and **periodic re-evaluation** as the project evolves. Handling these well helps Solution Architects deliver projects that are **efficient, effective, and aligned with client goals** — on time, within budget, and at the right quality.
