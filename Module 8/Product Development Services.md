# Product Development Services (PDS)

> [!note] Overview
> Product Development Services (PDS) is a **groundbreaking evolution** in strategic engagement models. It transforms the outsourced vendor from a simple **implementer** into a ==collaborative product co-creator==. In PDS, the contract is **outcome-based**, incentivizing the vendor to continuously **optimize and innovate** the product post-release.

## 1. Team Construction

The vendor assembles a dedicated software development team.

> [!info] Team Composition
> - **In-house talents** from the vendor
> - **Domain experts** sourced as needed
>
> The vendor **autonomously manages** the team and plans work according to the agreed **development roadmap**.

## 2. Solution Providing

Client and vendor collaborate to define the product.

> [!info] Joint Solution Definition
> - Develop the **product concept**
> - **Test business models**
> - Establish the **value proposition**
> - Make critical decisions on **technology stack, architecture, and milestones**

## 3. Development Process

Active collaboration continues during development.

> [!info] Process Properties
> - **Agile and multi-faceted**
> - **Flexibility** in responding to feedback and validation
> - **Careful planning** of project milestones for efficient progress

## 4. Post-Release Services

The vendor's involvement does not end at launch.

> [!info] Ongoing Responsibilities
> - Working on **new releases**
> - Maintaining **service delivery teams** for continuous product operation
> - Addressing **technical issues**
> - Managing IT infrastructure **configuration and monitoring**
> - Upholding agreed ==Service Level Agreements (SLAs)==
> - Ensuring **seamless product accessibility** for users

## PDS Phases at a Glance

| Phase | Activities | Vendor's Role |
|---|---|---|
| Team Construction | Assemble dedicated team | Autonomous staffing & management |
| Solution Providing | Concept, business models, tech decisions | Co-creator |
| Development | Agile build with feedback loops | Active builder |
| Post-Release | New releases, ops, SLAs | Long-term operator |

## 5. PDS vs. Staff-Augmentation-Based Outsourcing

> [!info] Key Difference
> Unlike **staff-augmentation-based** outsourcing where vendor involvement may be limited to specific phases, PDS provides **comprehensive coverage** across the entire IT product lifecycle — from **conceptualization** and **technology stack selection** to **development, service delivery, and ongoing maintenance**.

| Aspect | Staff Augmentation | Product Development Services |
|---|---|---|
| Scope | Phase-specific | End-to-end lifecycle |
| Vendor Role | Resource provider | Co-creator + operator |
| Contract Basis | Time & Materials | **Outcome-based** |
| Innovation Incentive | None | Built into payment |
| Relationship Depth | Transactional | Strategic, long-term |

## 6. Why PDS Fosters Deeper Engagement

> [!tip] Strategic Reasons
> - **Outcome-based contract** aligns incentives.
> - **Early collaboration** starts at concept, not at implementation.
> - **Vendor accountability** for the product's continued success.
> - **Clients gain a partner**, not just a supplier.

## Decision Hints

> [!example] When to Choose PDS
> - You want a **partner who shares product risk and reward**.
> - You need **innovation and continuous improvement**, not just delivery.
> - The product's **business model is still evolving**.
> - You prefer **outcome-based pricing** over hourly billing.

## Summary

Product Development Services is an end-to-end engagement model where the vendor acts as a ==product co-creator==, not just an implementer. The four phases — **team construction, solution providing, development, and post-release services** — span the full product lifecycle. The **outcome-based contract** drives continuous optimization, and the relationship starts at the earliest product stages, creating a far deeper partnership than traditional staff-augmentation models.
