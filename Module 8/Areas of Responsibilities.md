# Areas of Responsibilities: Presales Roles and Interactions

> [!note] Overview
> Presales is a ==team effort==. It requires a clear understanding of each member's role, responsibilities, and interactions. Four groups participate: the **client**, the **sales team**, the **presales team**, and **other architects** within the company. Each plays a distinct part in turning client needs into a feasible, well-priced solution.

## 1. Client Responsibilities

The client initiates and shapes the engagement by sharing what they need.

> [!info] Client Duties
> - Provide **RFI** (Request for Information)
> - Provide **RFP** (Request for Proposal)
> - Provide **sales decks** and supporting materials
> - Outline ==requirements and expectations== for the project

These documents form the input the vendor must respond to.

## 2. Sales Team Responsibilities

The sales team acts as a ==link between the client and the solution architects==. They focus on commercial aspects and may lack deep technical or business-domain knowledge.

### Key Tasks
> [!info] Sales Duties
> - Share client needs with the presales team
> - Understand **project timelines and pricing**
> - Review documents before they are sent to the client
> - Maintain the client relationship

> [!tip] Bridging Role
> Sales translates the conversation in both directions — client to architects and architects to client — without owning the technical solution itself.

## 3. Presales Team Responsibilities

The presales team owns the proposal. It is typically organized into three responsibility areas:

### 3.1 Project Management / Delivery Management
> [!info] PM/Delivery Duties
> - Understand project requirements
> - Outline deliverables
> - Assign resources
> - Manage timelines
> - Prepare contracts

### 3.2 Content Development
Creates the actual artifacts the client sees and evaluates.

> [!info] Content Duties
> - Presentation decks
> - Visualizations and diagrams
> - Delivery approaches
> - Estimations
> - Validation files
> - Ensure ==alignment with client requirements== and relevant experience

### 3.3 Integration and Quality Control
Ensures the proposed technical solution is realistic and meets standards.

> [!info] Integration & QC Duties
> - Involve **DevOps** experts
> - Involve **QA** experts
> - Validate feasibility of proposed technical solutions
> - Confirm quality standards are achievable

## 4. Other Architects in the Organization

The presales team also taps into broader architectural expertise inside the company to answer complex questions and produce ==comprehensive solutions==. These architects provide deep specialist input on demand.

## Responsibility Matrix

| Group | Primary Focus | Key Outputs |
|---|---|---|
| Client | Define needs | RFI, RFP, sales decks |
| Sales | Bridge client ↔ architects | Pricing, timelines, doc review |
| PM / Delivery | Coordinate effort | Deliverables, resources, contracts |
| Content Development | Build the proposal | Decks, visuals, estimations |
| Integration & QC | Validate feasibility | DevOps & QA sign-off |
| Other Architects | Specialist depth | Complex-question answers |

## How They Interact

> [!example] Collaboration Flow
> - **Client → Sales**: shares RFI/RFP/sales decks.
> - **Sales → Presales Team**: relays needs, timelines, pricing constraints.
> - **Presales Team (PM) → Content Development**: assigns work, sets deadlines.
> - **Content Development ↔ Integration & QC**: aligns proposed solution with technical reality.
> - **Presales Team ↔ Other Architects**: pulls in deep expertise for complex topics.
> - **Sales → Client**: delivers reviewed, polished proposal back.

## Why Clear Responsibilities Matter

> [!tip] Strategic Reasons
> - Avoids gaps and duplicated work across overlapping roles.
> - Ensures the sales team does not over-promise on technical solutions.
> - Lets specialists (DevOps, QA, architects) focus where their expertise adds the most value.
> - Produces a comprehensive, internally consistent proposal.

## Summary

Successful presales requires four collaborating groups — **client, sales, presales team, and other architects** — each with clearly defined duties. The presales team itself splits into **PM/Delivery, Content Development, and Integration & QC**, while sales bridges the technical and commercial sides. Close collaboration across all groups ensures the proposed solution truly aligns with client needs and is feasible to deliver.
