# Presales In Team: Roles, Support and Tools

> [!note] Overview
> Every presale effort is led by a single ==chosen leader== — often a Solution Architect, Delivery Lead, Delivery Head, or Business Analyst. The exact title matters less than the **skills and ability to lead presales work**. Around this leader, four supporting areas combine to ensure presales runs smoothly: the **architectural division**, the **sales team**, **research support**, and **special tools**.

## 1. The Presales Leader

A single person is appointed to manage the presales process end-to-end.

### Possible Titles
> [!info] Common Leader Roles
> - Solution Architect
> - Delivery Lead
> - Delivery Head
> - Business Analyst

### What Matters
> [!tip] Focus on Skills, Not Titles
> The title is secondary. What counts is the leader's ability to:
> - Coordinate diverse contributors
> - Drive the proposal forward
> - Synthesize technical and business input
> - Communicate confidently with the client

## 2. The Four Supporting Areas

Around the appointed leader, four pillars provide the expertise, capacity, and tooling needed to win the deal.

### 2.1 Architectural Division
The leader leverages the ==architectural team==, including:

> [!info] Architecture Contributors
> - **Solution Architects** — design and review the proposed solution
> - **Presale Consultants** — share experience from prior bids and improve proposals

They provide **guidance and valuable experience** that strengthens the proposal's technical depth and credibility.

### 2.2 Sales Team
The presales leader works closely with sales to ensure ==alignment between technical solutions and client needs==. Sales brings:
- Client relationship history
- Commercial framing
- Negotiation experience
- Understanding of the buying process

### 2.3 Research Support
Research support helps validate assumptions and uncover information needed for accurate, targeted proposals. This includes domain studies, technology benchmarks, and competitive intelligence.

### 2.4 Special Tools
Many organizations provide specialized tools to aid presales activities.

> [!example] Common Presales Tools
> - **Knowledge bases** — store past presales information for reuse
> - Proposal templates and asset libraries
> - Estimation tools
> - CRM and proposal-tracking systems

These tools prevent reinventing the wheel and accelerate response times.

## Supporting Areas at a Glance

| Pillar | Who / What | Value Provided |
|---|---|---|
| Architectural Division | Solution Architects, Presale Consultants | Technical guidance, experience, proposal quality |
| Sales Team | Account managers, sales reps | Client alignment, commercial framing |
| Research Support | Analysts, domain experts | Validated data, market insight |
| Special Tools | Knowledge bases, templates | Reuse, speed, consistency |

## Why This Structure Works

> [!example] Strategic Reasons
> - **Single point of accountability** — one leader avoids fragmented ownership.
> - **Specialized contributions** — each pillar brings expertise the leader cannot have alone.
> - **Reusable knowledge** — tools and consultants prevent starting from scratch each bid.
> - **Sales–technical alignment** — close collaboration avoids over- or under-promising.

## Summary

A successful presales engagement is run by a single appointed leader — regardless of title — backed by **four supporting pillars**: the architectural division, the sales team, research support, and specialized tools. This structure combines accountability with deep expertise, ensuring that proposals are technically sound, commercially viable, and grounded in past experience.
